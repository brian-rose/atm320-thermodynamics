Course description (from the Undergraduate Bulletin):

Equation of state; principles of thermodynamics; water vapor and moist air thermodynamics; changes of phase and latent heat; hydrostatic equilibrium; atmospheric convection; thermodynamic diagrams; atmospheric stability and severe weather events.

Course Objective:

The goal of this course is to understand the physical laws that govern how energy and heat are transmitted in the atmosphere.

Textbooks and reading

The required text is A First Course in Atmospheric Thermodynamics by Grant W. Petty, Sundog Publishing, 2008.

The course will closely follow this book, and students will be reading it cover-to-cover. Having your own copy is highly recommended. One copy will be on reserve at the Science Library.

Reading assignments will be clearly posted on the schedule page of the course website. The reading is required, and must be completed before class on the day indicated. It is important for every student to keep up with this schedule.

Students may also want to consult the excellent book Atmospheric Science: An Introductory Survey, 2nd ed. by J. M. Wallace and P. V. Hobbs, Academic Press 2006 for supplementary reading.

Quizzes and Exams

To encourage students to keep up with the assigned reading schedule, a pop quiz will occasionally be given in class. Dates will not be announced in advance. Quizzes will be given in the first 5 minutes of class.

There will be two in-class exams and a final exam during the University exam period (dates posted on the schedule page of the course website).
